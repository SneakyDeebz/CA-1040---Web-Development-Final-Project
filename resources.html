🔗 Student Resources

A curated list of websites and tools every college student should know about.

Academic Research Tools

Use these sites to find reliable sources for papers, projects, and research assignments.

Website Description Best For
Google Scholar Search engine for academic papers, theses, and citations across many disciplines. Research papers, citations
JSTOR Digital library of academic journals, books, and primary sources. Humanities, social sciences
PubMed Free database of biomedical and life sciences research from the National Library of Medicine. Biology, health, medicine
Wikipedia Good starting point for background knowledge — but always follow up with primary sources. Background, overview
Wolfram Alpha Computational knowledge engine — great for math, science, and data questions. Math, science, data

Free Productivity & Study Tools

Tool Description Type
Quizlet Create and study digital flashcards. Also has millions of pre-made study sets. Flashcards / Memorization
Khan Academy Free video lessons and practice exercises for math, science, history, and more. Video Lessons
Grammarly Writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, clarity, and style. Writing Aid
Notion All-in-one workspace for notes, to-do lists, and project planning. Free for students. Note-taking / Organization
Google Calendar Manage your class schedule, deadlines, and study sessions in one place. Scheduling
Desmos Free online graphing calculator — great for algebra, pre-calc, and calculus courses. Math Tool

University & General Links

  • University of Minnesota Crookston (crk.umn.edu) — Official university website
  • Google — General-purpose search engine
  • Chegg — Textbook rentals, tutoring, and homework help
  • Rate My Professors — Read student reviews of professors before registering
  • Federal Student Aid (studentaid.gov) — FAFSA, loans, grants, and scholarship info
  • Coursera — Free and paid online courses from top universities worldwide
💡 Tip: Most universities also offer free access to research databases like EBSCOhost and ProQuest through the library. Log in with your student ID to access them!
